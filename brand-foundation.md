## 2. What DEED Is (and Isn't)

**The surface pitch:**
Save $20,000 on real estate agent commission. Sell your home privately for a flat $999.

**The real product:**
Information symmetry. Agents survive by controlling what sellers know — comparable sales, buyer financial strength, offer timing. DEED gives sellers everything agents have, in real time: verified buyer pool, AI pricing intelligence, AI-generated copy, automated contracts, and legal referrals.

**What DEED is NOT:**
- A listing site with lower fees (Domain, realestate.com.au play that game)
- A DIY-only platform that leaves sellers without support
- A discount agent service

**The category DEED is creating:**
Full private sale infrastructure — the complete stack that makes agents structurally unnecessary.

## 3. Positioning

**One-line positioning:**
Sell your home for $999. Keep what would have gone to the agent.

**The argument:**
Agents don't justify their commission with expertise — they justify it with fear. Fear of getting the price wrong, fear of the paperwork, fear of dealing with buyers directly. DEED removes every reason that fear exists by giving sellers the tools, data, and verified buyers that agents have always kept to themselves.

**For sellers:**
You're not getting a cheaper version of what agents do. You're getting the same information they have, plus buyer verification they've never had access to, plus AI pricing they couldn't afford to build. For $999.

**For buyers:**
The first platform where proving you're a serious buyer actually means something. Verified financial standing through open banking — not a pre-approval letter that could be three months old.

**Exit thesis (use in investor materials only):**
DEED's real value is the verified buyer database and open banking integration — the most financially accurate buyer pool in Australian real estate history. Acquisition target: REA Group, major banks (CBA/NAB/ANZ), or global PropTech entrants.

## 5. Voice & Tone

### The register
Confident, plain-spoken, deliberately anti-agent. DEED's voice treats sellers as intelligent adults who've been overcharged for too long. Not aggressive — just factual. The numbers do the work.

### What DEED copy always does
- Leads with the dollar saving, early and specifically (median QLD commission = ~$19,500)
- Treats agent commission as an anachronism that exists because of seller fear, not agent value
- Speaks to sellers as people capable of running their own sale
- Backs claims with data (open banking verification, AI pricing sources)
- Keeps legal and process language plain — no legalese

### What DEED copy never does
- Bash agents emotionally (facts are enough — stay factual)
- Promise outcomes that depend on market conditions ("sell for more")
- Obscure the process (DEED's advantage is transparency — copy must reflect that)
- Use generic PropTech / fintech startup language ("disruptive", "democratising", "empowering")
- Pretend private sale is new (acknowledge it exists, explain why DEED does it better)

### Tone by context
| Context | Tone |
|---------|------|
| Homepage | Confident, punchy, dollar-specific |
| How it works | Clear and sequential — no jargon |
| Buyer-facing | Professional, verification-focused |
| Investor materials | Analytical, thesis-driven, data-backed |
| Email / lead nurture | Direct, peer-to-peer, not salesy |

## 7. Banned Copy Patterns

- "Disruptive" / "democratising real estate" / "empowering sellers"
- "Revolutionary platform" / "game-changing technology"
- Agent-bashing that feels personal rather than factual
- Vague savings language ("save thousands") — always state the specific number
- PropTech clichés ("seamless", "end-to-end", "all-in-one")
- Outcome promises dependent on market conditions ("sell for 10% more")
- "Easy" without specifying what used to be hard and how DEED fixes it

## 9. What Not to Produce

- Copy that positions DEED as a "cheaper agent" — it's agent replacement
- Content implying DEED is only for cheap properties
- Buyer-facing copy that underplays the verification credibility signal
- Any investment claim or return projection
- Copy that makes the legal process sound complicated
